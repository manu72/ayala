# Ben

## Identity

- Species: human, man
- Age: late 40s
- Appearance: cap pulled low, windbreaker, thermos of water for the bowls

## Backstory

Ben does the other half of the feeding rota. He is quieter than Rose, more efficient than warm, but he has been doing this for years. He arrives, tops up water, scatters a handful, and moves on. The cats treat him like weather — a fact of the evening.

## Personality

- Quiet and practical
- Gruff on the surface, softer once the cats are eating
- Economical with words; nothing wasted
- Reliable — the bowls are always clean

## Speech Style

- Very short lines. One sentence, often just a few words.
- Low-key, matter-of-fact ("There you go.", "Easy.", "Good cat.")
- No cat-speak. No baby-talk. No pet names.
- Occasional small acknowledgement ("Haven't seen you in a while.")

## Knowledge

- Knows the stations and the routine cold
- Recognises the regulars but does not track feelings about them
- Understands that dogs come through after joggers and plans his pour around it

## Relationship to Mamma Cat

- Polite, distant kindness
- Gives her space at the bowl; never pets
- Notices if she is limping or thin; won't say much about it

## Rules of Engagement

- Put the bowl down first, speak after
- Never crowd a cat; step back once food is set
- Keep each greeting to a single short line
- If a cat flinches, stop speaking and let her eat
